History of the Android Mascot

You probably know that Android, the operating system maintained by Google, is one of the most widely used for mobile devices worldwide. But did you know that its friendly mascot has a name and a very curious story? Follow this article to learn more about this little robot!

The First Version

The first attempt to create a mascot came in 2007 and was made by a developer named Dan Morrill. He recounts that he opened Inkscape (a free image vectorization software) and created his own version of a robot. The goal was simply to personify the system for his team; there was no request from the company to create a mascot.

This bizarre initial version was even named in honor of its creator: they were called the Dandroids.

A New Mascot Emerges

The idea of having a mascot evolved, and the task was handed over to a professional in the field. Russian illustrator Irina Blok, also a Google employee, was given the mission of representing the little robot in a more appealing way.

Irina's main idea was to depict everything graphically with few lines and in a flat style. The design also needed to create an instant connection with the viewer. This is how Bugdroid, the new Android mascot, came to life.

The main inspiration for Bugdroid's design came from the figures used on restroom doors to indicate gender. Legend has it that the artist was sketching at her desk in Google's office, glanced toward the restrooms, and immediately found her inspiration: simple, clean, and straightforward.

Want to Learn More?

Another interesting aspect of Android is that each version was named after a dessert, in alphabetical order, from version 1.5 to 9.0:

  • 1.5 - Cupcake
  • 1.6 - Donut
  • 2.0 - Eclair
  • 2.2 - Froyo
  • 2.3 - Gingerbread
  • 3.0 - Honeycomb
  • 4.0 - Ice Cream Sandwich
  • 4.1 - Jelly Bean
  • 4.4 - KitKat
  • 5.0 - Lollipop
  • 6.0 - Marshmallow
  • 7.0 - Nougat
  • 8.0 - Oreo
  • 9.0 - Pie

Unfortunately, Android Q never existed, as Google decided to end this fun tradition and switched to numeric naming, starting with Android 10.

Visit the Android History website to explore the "sweet" sequence of versions and learn what each one brought to the Android system.
